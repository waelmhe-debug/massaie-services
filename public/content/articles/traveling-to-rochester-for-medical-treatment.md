Rochester, Minnesota is home to the world-renowned Mayo Clinic — consistently ranked among the top hospitals on the planet. Every year, thousands of international patients make the journey to receive world-class medical care. This guide will help you navigate your trip with confidence.

## Why Rochester for Medical Treatment?

Mayo Clinic's reputation is unmatched. With specialists across virtually every field of medicine, cutting-edge research, and a deeply patient-centered approach, it attracts patients from over 140 countries annually. The city itself has grown around this institution, making it uniquely equipped to support international medical travelers.

Beyond Mayo Clinic, Rochester is home to several other highly regarded medical facilities and specialist clinics, making it a true hub for medical tourism.

## Planning Your Arrival

### How Far in Advance Should You Book?

We recommend reaching out to your concierge service **at least 4–6 weeks before your travel date**. This gives adequate time to:

- Confirm your appointment schedule
- Arrange suitable accommodation near your medical facility
- Coordinate airport transfers and transportation
- Prepare any documentation requirements

If your situation is urgent, our team can often arrange same-week support.

### Getting to Rochester

Rochester International Airport (RST) offers direct connections from major hubs including Chicago, Minneapolis, and Dallas. For international travelers, the most common route is:

1. **International flight** to Chicago O'Hare (ORD) or Minneapolis-St. Paul (MSP)
2. **Connecting flight** to Rochester (RST) — approximately 45 minutes
3. **Alternatively**, a private car transfer from Minneapolis takes approximately 1.5 hours

## Accommodation Options

Rochester offers a wide range of accommodation to suit every preference and budget:

- **Luxury hotels and apartments** within walking distance of Mayo Clinic
- **Extended-stay suites** ideal for stays of 2+ weeks
- **Private furnished apartments** for families accompanying a patient
- **Proximity-matched housing** coordinated through concierge services like Massaie

When selecting accommodation, consider your mobility needs, proximity to your primary medical facility, and whether family members will be accompanying you.

## What to Expect During Your Stay

### Daily Routine

If you are undergoing treatment, your days will typically follow a structured schedule around your appointments. A concierge service coordinates your daily transport to and from the medical facility, handles any scheduling changes, and ensures you have everything you need between appointments.

### Cultural Considerations

Rochester is a welcoming, multicultural city. However, if cultural or dietary requirements are important to you, planning ahead makes a significant difference. Massaie Services staff are trained in cultural sensitivity and can:

- Source culturally appropriate food and groceries
- Connect you with community prayer facilities
- Facilitate language translation when needed
- Assist with halal, kosher, or other dietary requirements

### Staying Connected

Upon arrival, we recommend obtaining a local SIM card. This ensures reliable communication with your medical team, family back home, and your concierge support team. Our team can provide a local SIM card as part of arrival services.

## Financial Planning

Medical treatment costs in the United States require significant advance planning. Beyond the medical bills themselves:

- **Accommodation** in Rochester typically runs $150–$500/night depending on options
- **Transportation** costs depend on frequency of appointments and distances
- **Daily expenses** including food and personal needs
- **Emergency reserves** for extended stays if treatment requires additional time

International patients should also investigate medical travel insurance, which can cover unexpected extensions to your stay.

## Preparing Your Documentation

Before traveling, ensure you have:

- Valid passport and appropriate US visa (B-2 Tourist/Medical visa is typical)
- All medical records, translated into English if necessary
- Insurance documentation and pre-authorization letters
- Emergency contact information and next-of-kin details
- A list of current medications (generic names, as brand names differ internationally)
